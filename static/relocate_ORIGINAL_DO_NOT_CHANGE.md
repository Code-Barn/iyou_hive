       2.Legal Basis and Request for Relief
       This case arises under the Illinois Parentage Act of 2015, and issues of parenting time and relocation are
       governed by Part VI of the Illinois Marriage and Dissolution of Marriage Act, including 750 ILCS 5/602.7
       (best interests of the child) and 750 ILCS 5/609.2 (relocation). Under 750 ILCS 5/609.2, the Court
       considers, among other things, the circumstances and reasons for the intended relocation; the reasons a
       parent is objecting; the history and quality of each parents relationship with the child; educational
       opportunities and extended family at the current and proposed locations; the anticipated impact of
       relocation on the child; and the Courts ability to fashion a reasonable allocation of parental
       responsibilities and parenting time while minimizing impairment to the parentchild relationship caused by
       relocation.
       Violettes recent suicidal ideation, inpatient psychiatric hospitalization, current intensive outpatient
       treatment, and the emergency Order of Protection based on Petitioners communications show that
       safety, stability, and mental health must be central to the relocation decision. Allowing Violette to relocate
       with me to a more stable home with immediate and extended family support, and to live at some
       distance from Petitioners volatility, is in her best interests. The Court can still preserve appropriate
       contact, if any is found to be safe, by fashioning a parenting schedule that reflects any restrictions or
       supervision orders entered under 750 ILCS 5/603.10. compliance with the 60day notice
       requirement.Because an emergency Order of Protection is in place and direct communication with
       Petitioner is restricted, I cannot safely provide ordinary written relocation notice outside of court. The
       intended move date of June 15, 2026 is less than 60 days after the May 1, 2026 return date on the
       emergency Order of Protection. Under these circumstances, strict compliance with the 60day notice
       provision of 750 ILCS 5/609.2(a) is impracticable. I therefore provide notice through this filed Motion and
       formal service and ask the Court to deem this sufficient or otherwise excuse strict compliance with the
       60day notice requirement.
       I also ask the Court to protect the confidentiality of my new residential address under 750 ILCS
       5/609.2(c) and in light of the Orders of Protection and Petitioners history of threatening and abusive
       behavior. I am willing to provide the address to the Court, the Guardian ad Litem, and any professionals
       or supervisors the Court designates, but I request that the address not be disclosed directly to Petitioner.
