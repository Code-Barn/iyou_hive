I. INTRODUCTION
This Motion is filed pursuant to the Judgment for Allocation of Parental Responsibilities (entered
August 3, 2022), specifically Section 21, 750 ILCS 5/602.5 (shared decision-making) and 750 ILCS
5/607(c)(1) (interference with parenting time).
Since December 2024, Respondent has willfully obstructed Petitioner’s right to communicate with
Violette, including:
      • Encouraging Violette to block Petitioner’s phone number, including on iMessage and other
        secure communication platforms.
      • Refusing to provide Violette’s phone number to Petitioner, despite repeated requests.
      • Failing to ensure Violette engages in communication with Petitioner, as required by Section
        21 of the Allocation Judgment.
Petitioner has made repeated, documented attempts to communicate with Violette, but Respondent’s
actions have prevented meaningful contact. Petitioner respectfully requests that this Court compel
Respondent to comply with the communication provisions of the Allocation Judgment and prohibit
Violette from blocking or refusing contact.

III. LEGAL ARGUMENTS
A. Respondent's Violation of the Allocation Judgment
     1. Violation of Section 21
            a) The Allocation Judgment explicitly grants both parents the right to communicate
            with Violette via telephone calls and video chat. Respondent has obstructed this right by:
                • Refusing to provide Violette's phone number.
                • Failing to ensure Violette engages in communication with Petitioner.
B. Respondent’s Violation of Illinois Law
      1. Interference with Parenting Time (750 ILCS 5/607(c)(1))
             a) This statute prohibits a parent from interfering with the other parent’s allocated
             parenting time and communication rights.
             b) Respondent has willfully violated this law by:
                 • Encouraging Violette to block Petitioner.
                 • Refusing to provide Violette’s phone number to Petitioner.
                 • Failing to ensure Violette engages in communication with Petitioner.
             c) Respondent’s refusal to allow secure communication interferes with Petitioner’s
             parenting time and violates this provision.
      2. Violation of Shared Decision-Making (750 ILCS 5/602.5)
             a) This statute requires both parents to facilitate and not obstruct the other parent’s
             relationship with their child.
             b) The Allocation Judgment grants both parents equal decision-making
             responsibility, including the right to communicate with the child.
             c) Respondent has obstructed this right by:
                 • Refusing to provide Violette’s phone number.
                 • Encouraging Violette to block Petitioner.
             d) Respondent’s actions constitute a clear violation of this statue, as they obstruct
             Petitioner’s right to communicate with Violette
      3. Emotional Abuse of Violette
             a) Respondent’s actions force Violette to choose sides and expose her to high-conflict
             dynamics, which is emotionally abusive and not in her best interests.
